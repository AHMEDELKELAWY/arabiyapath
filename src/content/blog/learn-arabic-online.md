## <a id="step-1"></a>Step 1: Choose the Right Arabic Type

This is the **#1 mistake** most learners make when they try to **learn Arabic language online**. They open a textbook, see Modern Standard Arabic (Fusha), and assume that's how everyone speaks.

It's not.

Fusha is used in news, books, and formal speeches. In daily life, people speak **dialects** — Gulf, Egyptian, Levantine, and more.

### Dialect vs Fusha — Which to Pick?

| Your Goal | Best Choice |
|-----------|-------------|
| Talk to people in the UAE, Saudi, Qatar | **Gulf Arabic (Khaleeji)** |
| Travel in Egypt or watch Arabic movies | **Egyptian Arabic** |
| Read the Quran, news, or formal texts | **Modern Standard Arabic (Fusha)** |
| Business in Dubai, Riyadh, Doha | **Gulf Arabic** |

If your goal is to actually **speak with people**, start with a dialect. Fusha is great later — but it's not the fastest path to real conversation.

## <a id="step-2"></a>Step 2: Master the Alphabet First

Yes, the Arabic alphabet looks scary. But here's the truth: **you can learn it in 1–2 weeks** with daily practice.

Tips that work:

- Learn **5 letters per day**
- Practice writing each letter by hand
- Use **flashcards** with native audio
- Focus on **recognition**, not perfection

Once you know the 28 letters, reading Arabic gets much easier because the language is mostly **phonetic** — what you see is what you say.

## <a id="step-3"></a>Step 3: Start with 100 Core Words

Forget 5,000-word vocabulary lists. The **100 most-used Arabic words** cover about **50% of daily conversation**.

Focus on:

- **Greetings** (hello, goodbye, thank you)
- **Numbers** (1–20)
- **Common verbs** (go, come, want, eat)
- **Family words** (mother, father, friend)
- **Question words** (what, where, when, how)

When you **learn Arabic online** with this focus, you start saying real things in your **first week** — not your first year.

## <a id="step-4"></a>Step 4: Use Native Audio Daily

Reading Arabic is one thing. **Hearing** it is another. Native audio trains your ear so you can:

- Understand real speech speed
- Copy correct pronunciation
- Pick up natural rhythm and tone

Any course that helps you **study Arabic online** should include native-speaker audio in **every** lesson. If yours doesn't — switch.

## <a id="step-5"></a>Step 5: Practice Speaking Out Loud

This is where 90% of learners fail. They read silently and never open their mouth.

**Fix it with these techniques:**

- Repeat every sentence out loud
- Record yourself and compare to native audio
- Talk to yourself in Arabic during the day
- Use **shadowing** — speak along with the audio in real time

Speaking out loud rewires your brain. It moves Arabic from "something you study" to "something you do."

---

## <a id="step-6"></a>Step 6: Build a Daily Habit (Even 15 Minutes)

Consistency beats intensity. **15 minutes a day** is far better than 3 hours once a week.

Try this simple schedule:

- **Morning (5 min):** Review yesterday's words
- **Lunch (5 min):** New lesson
- **Evening (5 min):** Speak out loud or watch a short clip

Anchor your Arabic study to something you already do — coffee, commute, or bedtime. Habit beats motivation every time.
