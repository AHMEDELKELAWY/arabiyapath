## Step 3 — The 12-Week Beginner Roadmap

Here is your full step-by-step plan. Follow it inside any quality **arabic online course**.

### Weeks 1–2: Sounds, Greetings, Survival Phrases

- Learn to recognize the **Arabic alphabet** (sounds, not perfect writing yet)
- Master 20 essential greetings: hello, goodbye, please, thank you, yes, no
- Practice basic introductions

**Example phrases:**
- مرحبا (marhaba) — Hello
- شلونك؟ (shlonak?) — How are you?
- اسمي… (ismi…) — My name is…

**Goal:** greet someone and say your name with confidence.

### Weeks 3–4: Numbers, Time, and Money

- Numbers 1–100
- Telling the time
- Prices and currencies

**Example:**
- بكم هذا؟ (bikam hadha?) — How much is this?
- عشرة دراهم (ashra dirham) — Ten dirhams

**Goal:** ask the price of anything and understand the answer.

### Weeks 5–6: Food, Coffee, and Restaurants

- Order in cafés and restaurants
- Common foods and drinks
- Polite requests

**Example:**
- أبغى قهوة من فضلك (abgha qahwa min fadlak) — I want a coffee, please
- الفاتورة لو سمحت (al-fatura law samaht) — The bill, please

**Goal:** order a full meal in Arabic without switching to English.

### Weeks 7–8: Directions, Taxi, and Travel

- Asking for directions
- Taxi vocabulary
- Locations: airport, mall, hotel, beach

**Example:**
- وين المطار؟ (wein el-matar?) — Where is the airport?
- على طول (ala tool) — Straight ahead

**Goal:** take a taxi anywhere in the city using only Arabic.

### Weeks 9–10: Family, Work, and Small Talk

- Talking about your job and family
- Where you're from
- Hobbies and weekends

**Example:**
- أنا من بريطانيا (ana min britanya) — I'm from Britain
- أشتغل في دبي (ashtaghil fi Dubai) — I work in Dubai

**Goal:** hold a 2-minute small-talk conversation.

### Weeks 11–12: Real Conversations and Review

- Combine all topics
- Long roleplays: shopping + taxi + café in one session
- Take your **beginner certificate quiz**

**Goal:** finish the beginner level of your **learn arabic online course** and earn your first certificate.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. How long does a beginner learn arabic online course take?

Most beginners finish a structured **learn arabic online course** in **8–12 weeks** with 25–30 minutes a day. To reach comfortable conversation, expect around **6–12 months** of consistent practice. The plan above gets you to confident beginner in 12 weeks.

### 2. Do I need to learn the Arabic alphabet first?

Not before you speak. The best **online arabic classes** teach you to **recognize** the alphabet while you learn useful phrases at the same time. Reading and writing fluency comes later — after you can already say 100+ phrases out loud.

### 3. Can I really learn Arabic online without a live teacher?

Yes. Millions become conversational with self-paced **arabic online course** programs. As long as your course has **native audio**, **daily speaking practice**, and a **clear path**, you don't need a live teacher to start. Add a tutor later for advanced practice.

### 4. Should beginners pick Gulf, Egyptian, or Modern Standard Arabic?

It depends on your goal. Pick **Gulf Arabic** to speak with people in UAE, Saudi, Qatar, Bahrain, and Oman. Pick **Egyptian** for Arabic media and travel. Pick **Modern Standard (Fusha)** to read news and formal texts. Don't try to learn two at once.

### 5. How much does a good learn arabic online course cost in 2026?

A high-quality **arabic online course** with native audio, structured lessons, and certificates typically costs **$15–$50 one-time** — far less than a single hour with a private tutor. Avoid courses with hidden subscriptions or robotic audio.

### 6. What if I miss a few days of my plan?

Don't quit — restart. Skipping 3 days won't undo your progress. Open your **online arabic classes**, do **one short lesson**, and rebuild the streak. Long-term consistency matters more than perfect daily attendance.
