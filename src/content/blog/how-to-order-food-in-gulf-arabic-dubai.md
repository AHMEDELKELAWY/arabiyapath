## How to Order Food in Gulf Arabic: A Simple Dubai Guide for Expats

### Direct Answer
If you live in Dubai, learning a few Gulf Arabic phrases will help you order food confidently. Simple words like greetings, polite requests, and numbers are enough to make everyday restaurant interactions smoother and more friendly.

---

## Why Ordering Food Is the Best Place to Start

For expats in Dubai, ordering food is one of the most common daily interactions. It's low pressure, repetitive, and practical — making it the perfect way to start speaking [Gulf Arabic](/learn/gulf-arabic).

Even using a few words shows respect and often leads to warmer service.

---

## Basic Gulf Arabic Phrases for Ordering Food

### 1) Greeting the Staff
- **السلام عليكم** (As-salaam alaykum) — Hello
- **هلا** (Hala) — Hi (casual)

### 2) Asking for the Menu
- **القائمة لو سمحت**  
  *Al-qaayma law samaht* — The menu, please

### 3) Ordering
- **أبغى** / **أبي** — I want  
- **أطلب** — I'd like to order

Example:
- **أبغى قهوة** — I want a coffee
- **أطلب شاورما** — I'd like to order shawarma

---

## Asking Questions About Food

- **إيش هذا؟** — What is this?
- **فيه دجاج؟** — Does it have chicken?
- **حار ولا عادي؟** — Spicy or normal?

These simple questions cover most ordering situations.

---

## Numbers You'll Actually Use

- **واحد** — One  
- **اثنين** — Two  
- **ثلاثة** — Three  

Example:
- **اثنين قهوة لو سمحت** — Two coffees, please

---

## Paying and Polite Phrases

- **كم الحساب؟** — How much is the bill?
- **شكراً** — Thank you
- **يعطيك العافية** — Thanks / Much appreciated

Using polite phrases leaves a very positive impression.

---

## Do You Need Perfect Arabic?

Not at all. Locals don't expect perfection. Clear pronunciation and polite effort matter more than grammar.

That's why a speaking-first approach works best.
